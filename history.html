About this course

The world has encountered countless transformations since the year 1000 in terms of policy, theory, technology, and society/cultural. From the first university established in 1088 to the Brown v. Board of Education in 1954 to the creation of the World Wide Web in 1990 to the year of the Massive Open Online Education in 2012 to today, the education sphere has been and continues to change. This historical timeline examines these global changes in order to help you see how we arrived here today, so check it out below!

COURSE TIMELINE
Week 1: 1000-1500

- 1088: First University (Bologna) founded in Italy *evolution of paper
- 1150: University of Paris
- 1167: University of Oxford
- 1436: Printing press by Johannes Gutenberg

Week 2: 1600s

-1636: Harvard was founded as the United States’ first high education institution.
-1642: Puritan Massachusetts passed a law requiring that every child be taught to read
-1658: Johann Amos Comenius created the first illustrated textbook in the Western world - Orbis Pictus. He saw schools as “slaughter houses of the mind.”
-1690: John Locke brought up the tabula rasa. Emphasized first-hand learning (field trips, images...) as opposed to old book learning
-1697: John Locke - On Working Schools (emphasizes need for work ethic)

Week 3: 1700s

-1751: Ben Franklin founded the first English Academy with more applicable classes like geography - later University of Pennsylvania
-1783-85: Noah Webster - A Grammatical Institute of the English Language - a book with three volumes. Led to spelling bee craze.
-1791: Bill of Rights passed - regarding the Tenth Amendment: “The powers not delegated to the United States by the Constitution, nor prohibited by it to the States, are reserved to the States respectively, or to the people.” Education = power of the state.
-unknown: Frederick the Great: Prussian model designed to create docile subjects and factory
workers (carried on to American schools in the 18th century) -unknown: Jacques Rousseau - children are innately good. Opposed existing teaching methods which emphasized that children differ from adults in quantity of knowledge. Wanted more individual student freedom in education.

Week 4: 1800s

-1806: Sandbox with monitors - Lancastrian method of schooling in NYC (form of edtech)
-1800-1830: U.S. teacher shortage. Used the “monitor” system in which a teacher would teach a small group of gifted students (monitors) and they’d go on to teach larger classrooms of their peers (self-governance). Led by Joseph Lancaster. Unpaid students teaching sometimes thousands of others
-1837: Oberlin was the first co-ed university
-1840: Sir Isaac Pitman pioneers the idea of correspondence learning
-1841: first Blackboard in classroom
-1863: First Land Grant College - Kansas State University
-1867: Department of Education is established

Week 5: 1900-1950s

-1900: Association of American Universities established - 63 research universities uplifting university standards
-1926: first SAT from the College Board
-1930: Overhead projector used by military then spread to schools
-1936: first programmable computer
-1939: First observed use of closed circuit television at the University of Iowa and at LA Public Schools
-1954: Brown v. Board of Education of Topeka (overturn Plessy v. Ferguson)
-1959: first ACT test

Week 6: 1960s

-1960: Sir Keith Murray established new schools with emphasis on science and engineering
-1960: University of Illinois created first “online” school before internet with linked computers
-1960s: Patrick Suppes and Richard C. Atkinson (Stanford professors) taught arithmetic and spelling to kids via computers
-1969: Open University is founded in the United Kingdom, pioneers multi-media distance learning with mailed materials including videos, audio, and texts - each student was assigned a tutor whom they spoke to on the phone in individual and group conversations
-1965: Elementary and Secondary Education Act - fed funds used to help low-income students
-1969: The Open Classroom by Herbert Kohl - about student-focused classroom

Week 7: 1970s

-1971: eBook invented (Michael Hart)
-1974: Equal Educational Opportunities Act is created; promotes equal protection and disallows discrimination

Week 8: 1980s

-mid-1980s: more than 300,000 students enrolled in university-level correspondence courses in the United States
-mid-1980s: more than 12% of Australian university students were enrolled via distance education programs

Week 9: 1990s

-1990: World Wide Web invented
-1991: Invention of the SmartBoard
-1993: Bill Nye the Science Guy
-1994: the Open University has more than 200,000 students from across Europe (including Russia, Hungary, Bulgaria), and offices across Europe in places like Belgium, Spain, and Greece
-1995: 690 degrees and 170 certificates are available via purely correspondence courses in the US
-1995: 57% of distance courses delivered via two way, interactive video in the U.S., 25% used other internet capabilities for instruction and learning
-1995: 70% of distance learning courses are developed by universities for their own use, 30% by external course development firms
-1998: students enrolled in traditional, physical institutions begin to supplement or ease their course load by adding online courses
-1999: 58% of American universities offered distance learning courses, with 753,640 students enrolled in them, 23% offered degrees available entirely with distance classes

Week 10: 2000s

-2002: 99% of American public schools have internet access
-2005: Youtube founded
-2006: First Bay Area Maker Faire
-2006: Stanford Online High School founded
-2007: Khan Academy Founded
-2008: first massive open online course (MOOC), a computing course based in Canada
-unclear: First co-learning space

Week 11: 2010-2019

-2010: US government publishes a National Education Technology Plan
-2011: Creation of Codeacademy
-2012: The year of the MOOC - edX, Udacity, and Coursera are all launched
-2014: Coursera reports $4 million in certification revenue since inception
-2015: Three Blue One Brown launches on Youtube
-2015: US government publishes Future Ready Schools: Infrastructure guide
-2016: Over 6.3 million U.S. students took on online class
-2016: Nearpod launches VR Education Experiences
-2019: Best section of ME 297: Forecasting for Innovators taught
